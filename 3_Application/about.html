Explanation of CO2 Emission Calculation

CO2 emissions are calculated by considering the energy consumed during the loading of a web page. This energy is converted into carbon emissions, as electricity is often produced by burning fossil fuels such as coal, oil, or natural gas, which release carbon dioxide into the atmosphere.

The process of calculating carbon emissions is based on the size of the web page, measured in bytes, and the energy consumed to load it, measured in kilowatt-hours (kWh). By knowing the amount of energy consumed, it is possible to estimate the carbon emissions produced during the loading of the page. The formula for calculating CO2 emissions is as follows:

CO2 emissions =
Page size × Energy per byte in kWh per byte × CO2 emission factor in kg CO2 per kWh

Where:

  • Page size is the overall size of the loaded web page, expressed in bytes.
  • Energy per byte is the average energy required to transfer one byte of data, expressed in kilowatt-hours per byte (kWh/byte).
  • CO2 emission factor represents the amount of CO2 emitted per kilowatt hour of energy consumed, expressed in kilograms of CO2 per kilowatt hour (kg CO2/kWh).

This analysis provides an estimate of the CO2 emissions associated with viewing a web page, helping to understand the environmental impact of surfing the Internet.
